Optical Lenses

Help You See Better

Optical lenses are transparent devices that refract light to correct vision problems, magnify objects, or redirect light. They come in various types each designed to address specific vision needs and improve visual clarity.

Single Vision

Correcting vision with one focal point, no peripheral distortion.

Bifocal

Correcting near and far vision with two distinct focal points.

Progressive

Seamlessly correcting near, intermediate, and far vision with gradual transition.

Contact Lens

Thin, flexible disc placed on eye to correct vision problems.

Hi Index

Thinner, lighter, and more comfortable lenses for strong prescriptions only.

Tinted

Colored lenses that reduce glare, enhance contrast, and protect eyes.

Here are some catchy options for specs frames:

A specs frame, or eyeglass frame, is the part of glasses that holds the lenses in front of the eyes. It's the basic structure of the glasses, and is often made from metal or acetate. The frame is made up of several components, including the bridge, hinges, temples, nose pads, and eyewire.

Full-frame

The frame that cover all around the lens.

Semi-rimless

Has a frame on the top and sides, but not the bottom

Rimless

No frame, lenses are held by drills or screws

Browline

Frames with a prominent upper edge

Aviator

Teardrop-shaped frames, often with double bridges

Round

Circular frames, often associated with vintage styles

Oversized

Larger frames that cover more of the face

Cat-eye

Frames with an upward curve at the outer corner

Sports

Frames designed for athletic activities, often with rubber grips
